E String

The E string is the sixth string, directly above the A string, and closest to you. By now you may have noticed that there are two "E" strings. One is uppercase, the one on this page, being the lowest, and the lowercase is the higher note.


  • The lowest E on the staff is played open.

  • The F is played by holding a finger on the first fret.

  • The G is played by holding a finger on the third fret.

  • The A is played by holding a finger on the fifth fret.

  • The B is played by holding a finger on the seventh fret.

  • The C is played by holding a finger on the eighth fret.

  • The D is played by holding a finger on the tenth fret.

  • The E is played by holding a finger on the twelfth fret.
