How to Hold a Guitar

Sitting

When sitting it is important to hold it in a way comfortable to you. Generally the curve in the guitar body will be rested on the right leg. This is for optimal holding, in which your left arm will be used around the neck. Generally when sitting you hold the guitar around the upper neck, near the first few frets. Your right arm will rest over the guitar, in a way that your hand can easily strum up and down over the sound hole.

This is however assuming you are a right handed guitarist. If you're not, flip directions (left leg rest, right arm for holding the neck, and left arm for strumming).

Images for reference will be added later!

Standing

When standing, it's hard to play if you don't have a strap. It is possible, but you will have to figure out how to hold it while playing. Without a strap, it is generally played by propping it on a leg. But straps are preferable, and when you are holding the guitar, it is generally the same as sitting, just minus resting on the leg. Again comfort is pretty important. If it feels super awkward it may not be right positioning, or it may affect your playing.
